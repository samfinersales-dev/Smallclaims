Most Common USCIS Form Rejection Reasons

The most common reasons USCIS rejects or returns immigration forms include missing signatures on the final page, incorrect date format (USCIS requires MM/DD/YYYY), name inconsistencies between fields, blank required fields that should say N/A, incorrect filing fee, missing initial on every page, wrong checkbox selections, and missing supporting documents listed in the form instructions.

How to Fill Out Form I-485 Correctly

Form I-485 Application to Register Permanent Residence is one of the most complex USCIS forms. Common I-485 mistakes include leaving Part 1 fields blank instead of writing N/A, inconsistent name spelling across sections, missing the required signature and date on Part 12, incorrect alien registration number format, and forgetting to include required initials on every page. The I-485 filing fee is $1,440 for most applicants, making errors extremely costly.

How to Fill Out Form I-130 Correctly

Form I-130 Petition for Alien Relative is used to establish a qualifying relationship with a foreign national relative. Common I-130 errors include mismatched names between the petitioner and supporting documents, missing proof of US citizenship or permanent residence, incorrect relationship category selected, and missing signatures. USCIS will return the entire package if the I-130 has errors.

Form N-400 Common Mistakes

Form N-400 Application for Naturalization is used to apply for US citizenship. Common N-400 mistakes include failing to disclose all trips outside the US in the past 5 years, incorrect continuous residence dates, missing tax return information, unanswered questions in Part 12, and missing the biographic information page. An N-400 rejection can delay your citizenship by 6–12 months.

Form I-765 Employment Authorization Common Errors

Form I-765 Application for Employment Authorization is frequently rejected for missing eligibility category codes, incorrect or expired supporting documents, missing passport-style photographs, wrong filing fee, and name discrepancies. Without an approved I-765 you cannot legally work in the United States, making accuracy critical.
